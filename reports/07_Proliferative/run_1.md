# Report: 07_Proliferative (run_1.md)

**Cell type:** Glioblastoma cells
**Disease:** Glioblastoma multiforme (GBM), malignant glioblastoma
**Tissue:** Brain, central nervous system

## Program Summaries
### Cell Cycle Checkpoint Control
Genes governing G1/S and G2/M phase transitions, spindle assembly checkpoint, and mitotic progression through CDK activation and cyclin-mediated regulation. Core cell cycle machinery dysregulated in GBM.

**Predicted impacts:**
- Uncontrolled G2/M transition and mitotic progression
- Shortened G2/M checkpoint, enabling rapid proliferation despite DNA damage
- Overriding normal cell cycle checkpoints driving malignant proliferation
- Enhanced genomic instability through checkpoint evasion

**Supporting genes:** CDK1, CCNB1, CCNB2, CDC25C, TTK, CDC20, CDCA2, CDCA8, RRM2

**References:** [web:11], [web:17], [web:2], [web:3]

**Biological processes:**
- CDK1/cyclin B1 activation: CDK1, CCNB1, CCNB2 (refs: [web:2], [web:11], [web:17], [web:38])
- G2/M phase control: CDC25C, TTK, WEE1, CDK1 (refs: [web:2], [web:3])
- S phase DNA synthesis: CDCA2, CDCA8, RRM2, CDC20 (refs: [web:32])

**Cellular components:**
- Centrosome/spindle pole: AURKA, ASPM, STIL, TPX2 (refs: [web:23])

**Reference notes:**
- Screening and authentication of molecular markers in malignant glioblastoma
- Construction and validation of cell cycle-related prognostic genetic model for glioblastoma
- Identification of CDK1, PBK, and CHEK1 as oncogenic signature
- Identification of potential oncogenes in glioblastoma

### Spindle Assembly Checkpoint
Coordinate regulation of kinetochore-microtubule attachment monitoring, spindle checkpoint complex assembly, and anaphase entry prevention during mitosis. Essential for accurate chromosome segregation.

**Predicted impacts:**
- Partial checkpoint override enabling mitosis with chromosomal errors
- Aneuploidy and chromosomal instability driving tumor heterogeneity
- Enhanced checkpoint activation in some cells leading to prolonged mitosis
- Unequal checkpoint fidelity across GBM cell population

**Supporting genes:** TTK, BUB1, BUB1B, KNL1, NDC80, SKA3, NUF2, CENPI, CDC20, PTTG1, ESPL1

**References:** [web:33], [web:35]

**Biological processes:**
- Kinetochore attachment monitoring: TTK, BUB1, BUB1B, KNL1 (refs: [web:33], [web:34], [web:35])
- Spindle checkpoint complex assembly: BUB1, BUB1B, MAD1, MAD2 (refs: [web:36], [web:37])
- Anaphase inhibition: CDC20, PTTG1, ESPL1 (refs: [web:37])

**Cellular components:**
- Kinetochore outer plate: KNL1, NDC80, SKA3, NUF2, CENPI (refs: [web:36])

**Reference notes:**
- Bub1 kinase in regulation of mitosis
- Distinct domains in Bub1 localize RZZ and BubR1 to kinetochores

### Aurora Kinase Signaling
Regulation of centrosome duplication, spindle pole organization, kinetochore function, and mitotic progression through Aurora A and B kinase activities. Critical for genomic stability.

**Predicted impacts:**
- Enhanced centrosome duplication driving mitotic abnormalities
- Improved spindle assembly efficiency enabling rapid cell division
- Increased genomic instability through supernumerary centrosomes
- Enhanced survival through AURKA-mediated immune checkpoint modulation (B7-H3, PD-L1)

**Supporting genes:** AURKA, AURKB, TPX2, NUSAP1, ASPM, STIL, RACGAP1, ECT2

**References:** [web:18], [web:19], [web:20], [web:21]

**Biological processes:**
- Centrosome maturation and duplication: AURKA, ASPM, STIL, CDK1 (refs: [web:18], [web:23], [web:25])
- Spindle assembly: AURKA, TPX2, NUSAP1 (refs: [web:21], [web:23])
- Cytokinesis and abscission: AURKB, RACGAP1, ECT2 (refs: [web:25])

**Cellular components:**
- Centrosome: AURKA, ASPM, STIL (refs: [web:23])
- Spindle midzone: AURKB, RACGAP1 (refs: [web:21])

**Reference notes:**
- Genetic variations in AURORA kinases associated with GBM
- AURKA inhibitor alters immune microenvironment
- AURKA kinase inhibition enhances NK cell-mediated cytotoxicity
- AURKA inhibitor alisertib with TPI 287 induce synergistic apoptosis

### Transcriptional Cell Cycle Regulation
Transcription factor networks controlling G1/S and G2/M phase gene expression, particularly FOXM1, E2F, and B-MYB pathways. Master regulators of cell cycle gene expression in GBM.

**Predicted impacts:**
- Constitutive G1/S and G2/M phase transcription programs
- Enhanced mitotic kinase expression independent of cell cycle phase
- Glioma stem cell maintenance through FOXM1-MELK axis
- Radioresistance through FOXM1-mediated pathways

**Supporting genes:** FOXM1, E2F7, MYBL2, MELK, CCNB1, CCNB2, CDK1, PTTG1

- G2/M phase gene expression: FOXM1, MYBL2, MELK, CCNB1, CCNB2 (refs: [web:43], [web:45])
- Mitotic kinase expression: FOXM1, MELK, PLK1, AURKA (refs: [web:45])

**Reference notes:**

- MELK-dependent FOXM1 phosphorylation in glioma stem cells
